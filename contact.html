About the founder
SG

Sanjey Gopinath

Sanjey Gopinath founded AssistiveTech Bridge with a clear conviction: that understanding accessibility and bioengineering should not wait until college or the workforce.

Students who learn early to design for everyone become the engineers, designers, researchers, and advocates who shape a more equitable world. Through school talks, workshops, and hands-on sessions, Sanjey is building a movement of young inclusive innovators, one classroom at a time.

The mission is personal: to give every student the same spark of excitement about assistive technology and bioengineering that drove the founding of AssistiveTech Bridge — the moment you realize that STEM isn't just about building things. It's about building a better life for everyone.

duPont Manual High School

Team contributor Sanjey Gopinath is a freshman at duPont Manual High School in Louisville, Kentucky — one of the most nationally recognized STEM high schools in the United States, known for rigorous academic programs and producing top-tier science fair competitors and researchers.

🔬

Kentucky Science & Engineering Fair

Sanjey has earned recognition at the Kentucky Science & Engineering Fair (KY-SEF), one of the most competitive state-level science and engineering competitions in the region — demonstrating a commitment to rigorous research and scientific inquiry from an early stage.

Thermo Fisher Junior Innovators Challenge

Sanjey has also received recognition through the Thermo Fisher Scientific Junior Innovators Challenge (JIC), a nationally prestigious middle school science competition — reflecting a strong research background, aspirations in biomedical engineering, and a deep interest in tinnitus and assistive technologies.
