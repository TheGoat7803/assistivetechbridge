What to expect

What a typical school visit looks like

1

Planning together

We work with teachers and organizers to align the session with class goals, timing, grade level, and student interests before the visit.

2

Framing accessibility

Students are introduced to accessibility, disability, and assistive technology in a positive, age-appropriate way that actively encourages questions.

3

Bioengineering examples

We walk through real devices and systems — from prosthetics and hearing tech to eye-tracking interfaces — connecting them directly to engineering principles.

4

Interactive reflection

Students reflect on who benefits, what still needs to be built, and how their own skills and passions might fit into this meaningful space.

5

Next steps

We share science fair ideas, clubs, online resources, and ways to keep learning so students can continue exploring on their own after the visit ends.
