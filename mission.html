Our Mission

Giving students a first look
at bioengineering and assistive technology.

AssistiveTech Bridge exists to help students understand how engineering, medicine, and accessibility come together — and to show them that they can be part of that work.

Why this matters

Helping students discover bioengineering and assistive tech

Most students learn science and math without ever seeing the technologies that turn those ideas into independence for real people. AssistiveTech Bridge was created to change that — to bring bioengineering and assistive technology into classrooms in a way that feels human, hopeful, and possible.

I started this because I want every student to have the opportunity I had: to see the intersection of engineering, medicine, and real human lives. When you understand how a prosthetic limb is designed, how a hearing aid processes sound, or how eye-tracking software gives someone a voice — STEM suddenly means something personal and powerful.

Through school talks and workshops, we introduce students to the field of bioengineering and assistive technologies. We show them how the concepts they study in biology, physics, and computer science map directly onto devices and systems that change people's lives every single day.

Our goal is to help every student walk away thinking: "I could do this. I could be part of building technology that includes everyone."

Who we serve

A bridge for curious students, teachers, and future innovators.

Students

We work with middle and high school students who are curious about STEM but may not yet know where they fit. We show them real examples from biomedical engineering and assistive tech so they can begin to imagine their own path in the field.

Teachers & schools

We partner with educators who want to bring real-world, human-centered STEM into their classrooms. Our sessions align naturally with science, engineering, biology, and technology coursework at the middle and high school level.

Future problem-solvers

We especially want to reach students who care about helping others. Assistive technology gives them a concrete way to see how design and engineering can reduce barriers, restore independence, and create opportunity for everyone.

Families & communities

As students learn about accessibility, they bring those conversations home — raising awareness and inspiring more inclusive thinking in their families, friend groups, and communities long after the session ends.

"When students see how assistive technology changes lives, STEM stops being abstract — and starts becoming personal."

— AssistiveTech Bridge
Core values

What guides every talk and workshop

01

Inclusion

We design everything with disability, accessibility, and representation in mind from the very start.

02

Curiosity

We encourage students to ask questions, challenge assumptions, and explore new ideas without fear.

03

Clarity

We break down complex bioengineering and assistive tech concepts into clear, relatable, visual examples.

04

Empathy

We center the voices and real experiences of people who use assistive technologies every single day.

05

Opportunity

We highlight real pathways — science fairs, clubs, coursework, and careers — that students can pursue right now.

06

Action

We want students to leave each session ready to take a next step — not just remember a presentation.
